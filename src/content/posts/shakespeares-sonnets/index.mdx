  scrawlUrl,
  text: texts[i],

It seems strange that one hand does so much more than the other. And yet it's clearly possible to train your non-dominant hand-- how many millions of [left-handed children were forced to write right-handed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Handedness#Discrimination_in_education)?

Well, I want to train my left hand of my own volition, and become a little more cultured at the same time. Shakespeare is widely recognized as one of the greatest English poets, but I had only ever heard one line of his poetry: "Shall I compare thee to a summers day?". Among his poems, are a collection of 154 sonnets. Each of these 14 line love poems are a perfect amount of text to copy everyday, and that's what I did for 154 days straight.

It's slow, messy, and I have no control. My hand burns with exhaustion and spasms from overexerting unused muscles. I have 154 days to change that.

For your viewing pleasure, I'm using a drawing tablet and my vibe-coded writing reanimation tool ([https://rescrawl.kylezhe.ng](https://rescrawl.kylezhe.ng)).

## Methodology

For the WPM calculations, a word is 5 non-space characters, and pauses longer than 2 seconds are excluded from the total time.
